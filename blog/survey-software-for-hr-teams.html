Survey Software for HR Teams: Engagement, Exit, and People Analytics

Definition

Survey Challenges for HR Teams

Anonymity credibility and social desirability. Employee surveys produce honest data only when employees genuinely believe their responses cannot be attributed to them individually. The credibility of anonymity depends on both the platform configuration (data anonymisation settings, suppression of small group sizes) and the communication around the survey. HR teams that deploy surveys through platforms that do not support genuine anonymisation, or that share results at team sizes below the minimum threshold, undermine the trust that drives honest engagement survey responses. Social desirability bias is particularly acute in organisations with low psychological safety, where employees fear retaliation for critical feedback.

Fragmented survey landscape across HR functions. Most HR functions run surveys across multiple touchpoints - annual engagement, quarterly pulse, onboarding at 30/60/90 days, exit, 360 feedback, manager effectiveness - using different tools for each. Engagement surveys in Glint, onboarding surveys in Microsoft Forms, exit surveys in a spreadsheet, and 360 surveys in a separate tool produces data that cannot be connected to identify the complete employee experience picture. Consolidating on fewer platforms, and ensuring consistent question wording where the same constructs are measured at different lifecycle stages, is a persistent challenge for HR operations.

Speed of survey deployment for HR programmes. HR calendar events - organisational restructures, leadership changes, office moves, layoffs, return-to-office mandates - often require rapid survey deployment to understand employee sentiment. The time required to manually program a 15-question pulse survey in Qualtrics is often the bottleneck preventing timely sentiment measurement. HR teams without dedicated survey programming resources are limited in their ability to deploy surveys quickly in response to organisational events.

Common Survey Types for HR Teams

Annual employee engagement surveys - comprehensive 30-60 question surveys covering the core engagement dimensions: pride, advocacy, commitment, manager effectiveness, role clarity, growth, wellbeing, and inclusion. Usually run annually with results shared to leadership and managers within 4-6 weeks of close.

Pulse surveys - 5-15 question surveys run quarterly or monthly to track specific engagement dimensions or monitor sentiment following organisational changes. Fast deployment and rapid results are the primary requirements.

Onboarding surveys - multi-wave surveys at 1 week, 30 days, and 90 days measuring new hire experience, role clarity, manager support, and early retention intent.

Exit surveys - structured feedback from departing employees covering departure reason, management quality, culture, and growth opportunities, with anonymity controls and departure reason category analysis.

360 degree feedback surveys - multi-rater surveys collecting structured behavioural feedback from manager, peers, and direct reports for individual development programmes and leadership assessments.

Manager effectiveness surveys - upward feedback surveys measuring direct report perceptions of manager behaviours across communication, development, recognition, and direction-setting.

Platforms HR Teams Use

Qualtrics EmployeeXM is the most comprehensive enterprise HR survey platform, supporting engagement surveys, pulse surveys, onboarding, exit, 360, and lifecycle surveys in a single platform with integrated people analytics dashboards. Used by large enterprises requiring advanced analytics and HRIS integration.

Microsoft Forms is widely used by HR teams in Microsoft 365 organisations for pulse surveys, onboarding surveys, and ad-hoc HR feedback where the simplicity of Teams integration outweighs the need for advanced analytics. Forms integrates with Power BI for results visualisation and Power Automate for HRIS-triggered survey distribution.

Glint (Microsoft Viva Glint) is an enterprise engagement survey platform used by large organisations running continuous listening programmes with real-time dashboard reporting and action planning features.

Culture Amp is widely used in tech companies and scale-ups for engagement, onboarding, and exit surveys with benchmarking against the Culture Amp platform database of employee experience data.

Google Forms is used by smaller HR teams and startups for simple surveys where advanced anonymisation and analytics are not required.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best survey platform for HR teams?+

Qualtrics EmployeeXM is the most comprehensive enterprise HR survey platform. Microsoft Forms is the most accessible for organisations using Microsoft 365, with Teams integration and Power Automate triggers. Culture Amp is widely used in tech companies. The best choice depends on your organisation's size, existing technology stack, and analytics requirements.

How do I ensure employee survey anonymity?+

Configure the survey platform to store responses anonymously (no respondent tracking), set minimum group size thresholds (typically 5-10 responses) before results are shown at the team level, communicate the anonymity approach explicitly before the survey, and ensure the survey distribution method does not create a link between the survey response and the specific individual.
