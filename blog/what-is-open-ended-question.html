What is an Open-Ended Question in Surveys? Definition and When to Use

Arun Raj•July 18, 2026•3 min read•Glossary
Definition

An open-ended question is a survey question that asks respondents to provide a free-text answer in their own words, without constraining the response to predefined options, enabling the capture of unexpected insights and nuanced opinions that closed-ended questions cannot access.

When to Use Open-Ended Questions

Open-ended questions are most valuable in three contexts. First, when the range of possible answers is unknown. Second, when the nuance and specificity of individual responses is more valuable than the aggregated pattern. Third, as follow-up questions to closed-ended items - particularly for NPS and CSAT, where asking "Why did you give that score?" provides the actionable detail the score alone cannot offer.

They are less appropriate when quantitative comparison is the goal, when respondents have low literacy, when the survey is deployed on mobile where typing is cumbersome, or when the research team lacks capacity to analyse qualitative responses.

Open-Ended vs Closed-Ended Questions

Closed-ended questions provide predefined response options and produce quantitative data that can be counted, compared, and trended over time. Open-ended questions produce qualitative data that reveals the reasoning behind scores, unexpected issues, and the language customers or employees use to describe their experiences.

Most professional surveys combine both: closed-ended questions for measurement and tracking, open-ended questions for diagnosis and insight. The typical structure is a rating question followed by one conditional open-ended question: "What is the main reason for your score?"

Analysing Open-Ended Responses

Open-ended response analysis ranges from manual thematic coding for small samples to automated text analysis for large datasets. Text IQ in Qualtrics, Microsoft Azure Text Analytics, and other AI text analysis tools categorise open-text responses into themes and sentiment labels at scale.

For NPS follow-up questions, the most common analysis approach is topic categorisation: grouping responses into themes to understand the drivers of Promoter and Detractor scores. This analysis is more actionable than sentiment scoring alone because it identifies which specific aspects of the experience are driving loyalty outcomes.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many open-ended questions should a survey have?+

Most surveys should have no more than two or three open-ended questions, and ideally one. Each open-ended question adds significant completion time and reduces response rates. For NPS surveys, one follow-up open-ended question is standard.

Do open-ended questions reduce survey completion rates?+

Yes. Open-ended questions consistently reduce completion rates compared to surveys with only closed-ended questions because typing a free-text response requires significantly more effort than selecting an option. This effect is larger on mobile devices.

How do survey platforms handle open-ended question analysis?+

Qualtrics provides Text iQ for automated thematic and sentiment analysis. SurveyMonkey and QuestionPro offer similar text analysis tools. For platforms without native text analysis, responses can be exported to CSV and analysed using Excel, Python, or specialist qualitative analysis tools.
