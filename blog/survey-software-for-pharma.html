Survey Software for Pharma: Clinical Trials, REDCap, and GCP Compliance

Arun Raj•July 18, 2026•3 min read•Glossary
Definition

Pharmaceutical survey automation converts clinical trial patient-reported outcome (PRO) instruments, investigator assessments, and site feedback surveys from protocol specification documents into REDCap, Qualtrics, and validated eClinical platforms - reducing transcription risk and accelerating deployment timelines while maintaining the instrument fidelity required by regulatory submissions.

Survey Challenges in Pharma

GCP and 21 CFR Part 11 compliance. Clinical trials conducted under Good Clinical Practice (GCP) guidelines and submitted to the FDA require electronic data collection systems that meet 21 CFR Part 11 requirements: audit trails, electronic signature support, access controls, and validated system documentation. Survey platforms used for clinical data collection must be either validated (with documented IQ/OQ/PQ) or assessed for Part 11 applicability under the FDA's risk-based approach. REDCap with appropriate institutional validation documentation is commonly used for academic and investigator-initiated trials; commercial sponsors typically use validated eClinical systems (Medidata Rave, Oracle InForm) for pivotal trials.

Patient-reported outcome (PRO) instrument regulatory requirements. The FDA's PRO Guidance requires that PRO instruments used as primary or key secondary endpoints in regulatory submissions be linguistically validated for the target population and cognitively debriefed with patients. The exact PRO instrument wording - including item order, response option labels, time frame references, and recall period - must match the linguistically validated version. Any deviation from the validated instrument requires regulatory justification.

Multi-site, multi-country deployment consistency. Phase II and Phase III clinical trials run across dozens to hundreds of investigative sites in multiple countries. Deploying the same PRO instrument consistently in each country-language version, across multiple site systems, with identical branching logic and scoring algorithms, requires careful instrument management. Version control becomes critical: if the protocol-specified instrument version changes between sites or time points, data cannot be pooled for analysis.

Common Survey Types in Pharma

Patient-reported outcomes (PROs) - symptom scales, quality of life instruments, and disease-specific outcome measures completed by patients as primary or secondary trial endpoints. Common instruments include EORTC QLQ-C30, EQ-5D, FACIT scales, BPI, and disease-specific PROs developed by the sponsor.

Clinician-reported outcomes (ClinROs) - assessor-rated instruments completed by investigators or site staff based on clinical observation or interview, including CDAI, HAMD, CGI scales, and structured diagnostic interview instruments.

Electronic patient diaries - daily or weekly symptom diaries completed by patients between site visits, often capturing symptom frequency, severity, and medication adherence data electronically via tablet or mobile device.

Site and investigator feedback surveys - operational surveys measuring site satisfaction with sponsor support, protocol clarity, and study conduct, used for site engagement monitoring and protocol amendment feedback.

Post-market surveillance surveys - patient and healthcare provider surveys deployed after approval to monitor real-world safety signals, adherence patterns, and patient-reported effectiveness of approved medicines.

Platforms Pharma Teams Use

REDCap is extensively used by academic medical centres and cooperative research groups for investigator-initiated trials and academic-sponsored studies. REDCap's data dictionary structure, audit trail, and survey module support many GCP requirements. Institutional validation documentation is required for Part 11 compliance.

Medidata Rave and Oracle InForm are the dominant validated EDC systems for commercial sponsor-initiated Phase II and Phase III trials requiring full 21 CFR Part 11 validation documentation. PRO modules within these systems handle patient-facing survey deployment.

Qualtrics is used for non-GCP survey data collection in pharma contexts: medical affairs surveys, healthcare provider research, patient advocacy organisation surveys, and internal staff surveys. Qualtrics is not typically used for primary clinical efficacy data collection in regulated trials.

ERT (Clario) and Medidata Patient Cloud are specialist eCOA (electronic clinical outcome assessment) platforms purpose-built for validated PRO data collection in clinical trials, with FDA-cleared device support and multi-language management.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is REDCap 21 CFR Part 11 compliant for clinical trials?+

REDCap can be configured to meet many 21 CFR Part 11 requirements - audit trails, access controls, and electronic signatures - but compliance depends on the institutional validation documentation package, system configuration, and operational SOPs. Institutions using REDCap for Part 11-regulated data collection should have a validated REDCap instance with documented IQ/OQ/PQ and appropriate SOPs.

What is the difference between PRO and ClinRO instruments?+

Patient-reported outcomes (PROs) are completed by the patient based on their own perceptions and experiences, without interpretation by a clinician. Clinician-reported outcomes (ClinROs) are completed by a trained investigator or site staff member based on clinical observation, interview, or assessment. Both are subject to FDA guidance on instrument development and regulatory use.
