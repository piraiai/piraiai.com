Best Tools to Convert Documents to Google Forms (2026 Review)

Arun Raj • June 8, 2026 • 2 min read

Google Forms has no import button. There is no native way to upload a Word document, PDF, or Excel file and have it become a form. Every tool in this space works around that limitation differently. Some require a Google Docs intermediate step. Some require a Workspace add-on. Some produce a different form entirely. Here is an honest breakdown of every major tool available in 2026 and which one to use for your use case.

The Tools: What Each One Actually Does

1. Pirai AI

Best for: Researchers, universities, agencies, and anyone who needs direct file upload without a Google Docs step.

Pirai AI converts Word, PDF, Excel, CSV, TXT, XML, and Qualtrics QSF files into a Google Apps Script that builds your form automatically. Upload your file at platform.piraiai.com, select Google Forms, download the script, paste it into the Google Forms Apps Script editor, and click Run. No Google Docs step. No add-on. No coding required. See the full guide to converting any document to Google Forms.

Google Forms question types supported: Multiple Choice, Multiple Answer (checkboxes), Matrix (multiple choice grid), Matrix Multiple Answer (checkbox grid), Single Line Text (short answer), Essay (paragraph), Dropdown, and Descriptive Block (form title and description). All 8 common Google Forms question types. For input file formatting guidance, see the Pirai AI Excel spec format.

Also outputs to: Qualtrics, REDCap, SurveyMonkey, Microsoft Forms, QuestionPro, Zoho Survey, Alchemer, Forsta, and 15+ other platforms from the same upload. See the full platform list.

Free for: Academic researchers, teachers, and students.

2. Formswrite

Best for: Educators who need to export to multiple LMS platforms alongside Google Forms.

Formswrite accepts PDFs, Word documents, and Google Docs and converts them to Google Forms and 25+ other platforms including Canvas, Moodle, Kahoot, Quizizz, and Blackboard. It is trusted by 41,000+ educators. The primary workflow requires a Google Docs link as input for Word content, which adds an intermediate step when starting from a .docx file. Strong for education and quiz-focused conversions.

Limitation: Primarily education-focused. Less suited to research surveys, clinical instruments, or enterprise questionnaires.

3. Doc2Form

Best for: Users who want Google-native AI and are comfortable granting Google Drive access.

Doc2Form uses Gemini AI to extract questions from PDFs and Word documents and build the Google Form directly in your Google Drive. It is fast for simple documents and requires no external platform. The free tier covers the first form only.

4. GETMARKED Digitaliser

Best for: Teachers converting Word quizzes and QTI files to Google Forms.

GETMARKED has processed over 17 million questions as of mid-2026 and is heavily education-focused. It converts Word documents and QTI files (the standard quiz format used by most LMS platforms) to Google Forms. Strong for quiz-style content with correct answers and scoring. Less suited to survey-style instruments without right/wrong answers.

Limitation: Quiz-focused. Does not support research questionnaire formats, validated instruments, or non-quiz survey structures well.

5. Automagical Forms

Best for: Users already working inside Google Workspace who want a fast conversion without leaving Drive.

Automagical Forms is a Google Workspace Marketplace add-on that converts PDFs, Google Docs, and images into Google Forms from inside Google Drive. It also exports to Canvas and other LMS platforms. Because it runs inside your Google account, it feels seamless for users already in the Google ecosystem.

Limitation: Requires add-on installation and grants access to Google Drive. Not available in organisations that restrict Workspace Marketplace installs. Works best with Google Docs and PDFs : Word file upload requires an extra step.

6. Form to Doc

Best for: Users who need two-way conversion : both creating Google Forms from documents AND exporting Google Forms to other formats.

Form to Doc is a Google Workspace Marketplace add-on that works in both directions: it converts plain text, documents, images, and web pages into Google Forms, and it exports Google Forms and responses to Docs, Sheets, PDF, Excel, and CSV. The two-way capability is unique in this category.

Limitation: Requires Workspace add-on installation. Works best within the Google ecosystem. Less suited to users who need to convert non-Google file formats or output to non-Google platforms.

7. Weavely

Best for: Users who want a fast, well-designed form but do not specifically need a Google Form.

Weavely converts Google Docs into forms quickly : but it creates Weavely forms, not Google Forms. If you need a forms.google.com link, Google Classroom integration, or Google Sheets response data, Weavely is not the right choice. For users who are flexible about the form platform, Weavely offers a modern, well-designed alternative.

Limitation: Does not produce Google Forms. Not suitable for users who specifically need Google Forms integration.
