Survey Software for Higher Education: Student Surveys, REDCap, and Qualtrics

Arun Raj•July 18, 2026•3 min read•Glossary
Definition

Higher education survey automation converts student experience instruments, course evaluation forms, and academic research surveys from Word or Excel documents into Qualtrics, REDCap, Google Forms, and other platforms - enabling consistent deployment across faculties, campuses, and research teams without manual re-entry by each unit.

Survey Challenges in Higher Education

Decentralised survey operations across faculties and campuses. Large universities have dozens of faculties, research centres, and administrative departments each running their own surveys - student experience surveys, course evaluations, research instruments, alumni surveys, and staff engagement surveys. Without centralised survey infrastructure, each unit builds its own surveys independently, producing inconsistent question wording, incompatible data structures, and duplicated programming effort across the institution.

IRB compliance and research instrument fidelity. Academic research teams in universities regularly deploy validated psychological, educational, and social science instruments that must preserve exact wording for IRB compliance and publication validity. Converting a validated 40-item instrument from a PDF publication or protocol document to a REDCap or Qualtrics survey without introducing transcription errors is time-intensive when done manually, particularly for instruments with complex branching logic or embedded scoring calculations.

Student privacy requirements (FERPA). Surveys that link responses to student educational records fall under FERPA protection in the US. Survey platforms must be configured to handle student identifiers appropriately, separating personally identifiable information from response data and ensuring that course evaluation responses are not accessible to faculty until after grades are submitted - a workflow constraint that requires specific platform configuration rather than default survey settings.

Common Survey Types in Higher Education

Course and teaching evaluations - end-of-term student feedback on teaching effectiveness, course design, and learning outcomes, typically deployed at scale across hundreds of sections simultaneously with automatic instructor-specific distribution.

Student experience and engagement surveys - institutional surveys covering belonging, academic support, wellbeing, and campus services, benchmarked against national datasets such as the National Survey of Student Engagement (NSSE) or the UK's National Student Survey (NSS).

Academic research instruments - validated psychological, educational, and social science surveys deployed under IRB or ethics board approval for faculty and postgraduate research projects across social sciences, health sciences, and education departments.

Alumni surveys - graduate outcome surveys tracking employment, further study, earnings, and satisfaction with university preparation, often required for accreditation reporting and institutional ranking submissions.

Staff engagement and culture surveys - periodic engagement surveys for academic and professional staff, benchmarked against higher education sector norms for turnover, workload, and management quality.

Admissions and enrolment surveys - surveys of accepted, enrolled, and non-enrolling applicants to understand decision factors and improve recruitment communications.

Platforms Universities Use

Qualtrics is the most widely deployed survey platform in higher education, with institutional site licences at hundreds of universities globally. Most universities have a centralised Qualtrics account managed by IT or the institutional research office, with faculty and researchers accessing surveys through the institution's single sign-on. Qualtrics' 360 and course evaluation templates are frequently customised for academic contexts.

REDCap is the standard platform for health sciences research, clinical trials, and any academic survey requiring IRB-grade audit trails and institution-controlled data storage. Universities with academic medical centres typically have separate REDCap and Qualtrics installations serving different research communities.

Google Forms is widely used for informal surveys, class feedback, event registration, and administrative data collection where institutional data governance requirements are not triggered. It is accessible to all Google Workspace users without additional licensing, making it the default choice for ad-hoc survey needs.

Microsoft Forms is used in universities running Microsoft 365 environments, particularly for staff surveys and course evaluation alternatives in institutions without Qualtrics licensing.
