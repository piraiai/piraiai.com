Definition

Survey Challenges for Nonprofits

Limited technical resources and budget constraints. Most nonprofits do not have dedicated survey programmers or CX teams. Programme officers, fundraising managers, and M&E (monitoring and evaluation) staff design and deploy surveys as part of broader roles. They typically work with free or low-cost tools, face steep learning curves with enterprise platforms, and have limited time for survey programming. The result is that surveys are often simpler than the organisation's information needs require, or are built inconsistently across programmes and geographies, making cross-programme comparison impossible.

Beneficiary literacy and language diversity. Nonprofits serving diverse populations face significant survey design challenges around literacy, language, and digital access. Surveys designed for educated English-speaking respondents may not be accessible to beneficiaries with limited literacy, limited English proficiency, or limited smartphone access. Survey instruments need to be adapted for the population being served - shorter, simpler, in local languages, and available in multiple modes (online, phone, paper) - which multiplies the survey management effort significantly.

Funder reporting requirements. Grant-funded nonprofits are required to collect and report specific outcomes data as a condition of funding. Different funders require different metrics, question formats, and reporting templates, creating a landscape of separate evaluation surveys per programme per funder. Managing these separate surveys, ensuring consistent question wording where the same outcomes are being measured, and aggregating results across funders for internal learning is operationally complex for small programme teams.

Common Survey Types for Nonprofits

Programme evaluation surveys - pre and post surveys measuring knowledge, attitudes, skills, and behaviours of programme participants before and after intervention, typically required for funder reporting and impact measurement.

Beneficiary satisfaction surveys - feedback surveys measuring programme participants' experience of services, staff, and outcomes, used for service improvement and trustee reporting.

Donor surveys - periodic surveys of donors measuring satisfaction with communications, understanding of impact, and likelihood to renew or increase giving. Often deployed annually or post-campaign.

Volunteer feedback surveys - post-volunteering surveys measuring volunteer experience, motivation, and likelihood to return, used for volunteer retention programme management.

Community needs assessment surveys - surveys of target communities measuring unmet needs, service gaps, and priorities to inform programme design and funding applications.

Staff and trustee surveys - internal surveys measuring team culture, workload, leadership effectiveness, and alignment with organisational mission, often required for governance reporting.

Platforms Nonprofits Use

Google Forms is the most widely used survey platform in the nonprofit sector due to zero cost, ease of use, and integration with Google Workspace for Nonprofits (which provides free Google Workspace access to eligible organisations). Google Forms works for simple surveys without complex branching or anonymisation requirements.

SurveyMonkey for Nonprofits offers discounted pricing for registered nonprofits and provides more advanced features than Google Forms including question branching, response limits, and basic analytics. Widely used for donor surveys and beneficiary feedback programmes.

Microsoft Forms is used by nonprofits using Microsoft 365 through the Microsoft Tech for Social Impact programme, which provides discounted Microsoft 365 access to nonprofits.

KoBoToolbox is widely used by international development nonprofits and humanitarian organisations for field data collection and beneficiary surveys in low-connectivity environments, with offline data collection support and ODK compatibility.

Qualtrics for Nonprofits provides discounted access for qualifying nonprofit organisations, and is used by larger nonprofits and social enterprises with sophisticated M&E requirements.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best free survey platform for nonprofits?+

Google Forms is the best free option for nonprofits with Google Workspace for Nonprofits access. SurveyMonkey offers discounted nonprofit pricing with more features than Google Forms. KoBoToolbox is free and widely used by international development nonprofits, particularly for field data collection in low-connectivity environments.

How should nonprofits measure programme impact through surveys?+

Use pre-post survey design: deploy the same outcome measurement survey before the programme begins and after it ends, then compare scores to measure change attributable to the intervention. Use validated outcome measurement tools from your sector where available (e.g. Warwick-Edinburgh Mental Wellbeing Scale for mental health programmes) rather than developing custom instruments, to enable benchmarking.
