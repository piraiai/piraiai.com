How to Write Market Research Survey Questions: Examples and Guide

Arun Raj•July 18, 2026•3 min read•Glossary
Definition

Market research surveys collect structured data about consumer attitudes, behaviours, preferences, and brand perceptions to inform strategic decisions about product development, pricing, positioning, and communications. Effective market research question design balances statistical rigour with respondent accessibility.

Essential Questions to Include

Market research surveys vary significantly by objective. Brand tracking surveys focus on awareness, consideration, and preference. Usage and attitude studies cover penetration, frequency, and driver analysis. Concept tests evaluate new product ideas. Here are the core question types for each objective.

Unaided brand awareness (brand tracking)
"Which brands of [category] come to mind?" (open text, multi-response)
Unaided awareness must be measured before aided awareness. Never mention brand names before this question.

Aided brand awareness (brand tracking)
"Which of the following brands of [category] have you heard of?" (multi-select from a list including all tracked brands)
Present aided awareness only after the unaided question to avoid contaminating the unaided measure.

Brand usage and penetration
"Which of the following brands have you purchased or used in the past 3 months?" (multi-select)

Brand preference
"Which one brand of [category] do you use most often?" (single-select from used brands via skip logic)
This "most often used" question is more actionable than a simple preference question because it captures primary brand relationship.

Attribute association (image and positioning)
"Which of the following brands do you associate with each statement below?" (matrix: brands as columns, attributes as rows, multi-select allowed per row)
This association battery identifies which attributes each brand owns in the consumer's mind.

Concept test rating
"How interested would you be in purchasing [Concept Description]?" (5-point scale: Definitely would not buy to Definitely would buy)
The "definitely would buy" top-box is the standard concept test purchase intent metric.

Question Wording Best Practices

For unaided awareness and usage questions, the category definition in the question stem must be specific enough to elicit the right competitive frame but not so specific that it primes brand mentions. "Which brands of premium bottled water come to mind?" is better than "Which brands do you think of in the still and sparkling water category?" because the former is a natural consumer frame; the latter is a category definition from a brand manager's perspective.

Purchase intent questions should describe the concept at the level of specificity that reflects what the consumer would actually know at the point of purchase decision. An overly detailed technical description inflates intent among engaged respondents; an overly vague description deflates intent for respondents who need to understand the value proposition before expressing interest.

Rotate brand order and attribute order in all brand battery questions to prevent order effects. Always-first brands receive slightly higher scores simply from appearing at the top of the list. Most platforms support randomisation at the answer level within a question.

Platform Configuration

Qualtrics: Use Loop and Merge for brand battery questions where respondents rate each brand on the same set of attributes - this presents one brand at a time on the same question block rather than a large matrix, reducing visual complexity. Use Randomisation blocks for brand and attribute order rotation. Set up quota management for demographic targets at the survey level.

Forsta: Forsta (formerly Confirmit) is widely used for large-scale quantitative market research. Use its native loop question structure for brand batteries and its built-in quota management for complex sampling designs. Forsta's scripting environment supports advanced market research logic including sequential monadic concept tests and MaxDiff exercises.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Asking aided awareness before unaided awareness. The order contamination from mentioning brand names before the unaided awareness question invalidates the unaided measure entirely. This is the most consequential question order error in brand tracking research.

Using purchase intent to predict actual purchase. Purchase intent scores consistently overpredict actual purchase rates. A "definitely would buy" response translates to approximately 70-80% actual purchase rates for established categories; less for new categories or high-involvement purchases. Apply category-specific conversion factors when using intent data for volume forecasting.

Not screening for category relevance. Collecting opinions about a brand from respondents who have never purchased or considered the category produces noise, not signal. Screen respondents for category involvement in the first section and exclude those outside the relevant target audience.

Confusing brand preference with brand loyalty. Preference is a stated attitude; loyalty is a behavioural pattern. Most often used brand is a better proxy for loyalty than a stated preference question because it reflects actual behaviour rather than social desirability.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long should a market research survey be?+

Commercial online market research surveys should be 15-20 minutes maximum for a general consumer audience. Brand tracking surveys are typically 10-15 minutes. Concept tests and U&A studies run 20-25 minutes for engaged respondents with category involvement. Surveys longer than 25 minutes show significant data quality deterioration in online panels.

What question types are most common in market research surveys?+

Single-select multiple choice (for awareness, usage, preference), multi-select multiple choice (for attribute associations), rating scales (for satisfaction, intent, importance), rank order (for prioritisation), and open text (for unaided awareness and verbatim feedback). Market research surveys rarely use Likert agree-disagree scales - rating scales with specific end labels are more common.

Should market research surveys use quotas?+

Yes. Most commercial market research uses quota sampling to control for age, gender, region, and category involvement. Define your quota cells before fieldwork and set hard quotas in the survey platform to prevent oversampling of easier-to-reach demographic groups.
