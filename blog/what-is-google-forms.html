What Is Google Forms? Complete Guide (2026)

Arun Raj • June 8, 2026 • 2 min read

Google Forms is a free online form builder created by Google. It lets you create surveys, quizzes, questionnaires, event registrations, and feedback forms : and share them with anyone via a link. Responses are collected automatically and stored in Google Sheets. It is part of Google Workspace and available to anyone with a Google or Gmail account at no cost.

What Is Google Forms?

Google Forms launched in 2008 as part of Google Docs. It became a standalone product in 2016 and has grown into one of the most widely used survey tools in the world : particularly in education, academic research, and small business.

How Google Forms Works

Creating a form in Google Forms is straightforward. Go to forms.google.com, click the plus icon to start a blank form, and add questions using the question type selector on the right. Share the form via a link, email, or embed it on a website. Responses appear in the Responses tab and can be linked to a Google Sheet for analysis.
