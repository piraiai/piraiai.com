What is Net Promoter Score (NPS)? Survey Design Guide

Arun Raj•July 18, 2026•3 min read•Glossary
Definition

Net Promoter Score (NPS) is a customer loyalty metric that measures the likelihood of customers recommending a product, service, or organisation to others, calculated by subtracting the percentage of Detractors (scores 0-6) from the percentage of Promoters (scores 9-10) on a 0-10 scale.

What NPS Measures

NPS was introduced by Fred Reichheld in a 2003 Harvard Business Review article and has become one of the most widely used metrics in customer experience research. It measures customer loyalty - specifically the willingness to recommend, which Reichheld argued was the single most reliable predictor of growth.

NPS is used across CX programmes, EX surveys, product feedback cycles, and brand tracking studies. It is deployed as a relationship survey (periodic, unprompted) or a transactional survey (triggered after a specific interaction). The distinction significantly affects how NPS scores should be interpreted and benchmarked.

The NPS Question

The standard NPS question is: "On a scale of 0 to 10, how likely are you to recommend [company/product/service] to a friend or colleague?"

The question must use a 0-10 scale with 0 labelled "Not at all likely" and 10 labelled "Extremely likely." Deviating from this wording or scale produces scores that cannot be benchmarked against industry NPS data. The follow-up open-text question - asking why the respondent gave that score - is optional but essential for actionable insight.

Calculating NPS

Respondents are categorised into three groups based on their score:

  • Promoters (9-10) - loyal enthusiasts likely to recommend
  • Passives (7-8) - satisfied but unenthusiastic, vulnerable to competitor offers
  • Detractors (0-6) - unhappy customers who may actively discourage others

NPS = % Promoters minus % Detractors. The result ranges from -100 to +100. An NPS above 0 is acceptable, above 50 is excellent, and above 70 is world-class.

NPS in Survey Platforms

Most professional survey platforms have a dedicated NPS question type. In Qualtrics, the NPS question type applies the correct scale labelling and integrates with dashboard widgets for live score tracking. In QuestionPro and SurveyMonkey, NPS is also a native question type with built-in analytics.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good NPS score?+

NPS benchmarks vary significantly by industry. Above 0 is generally acceptable, above 50 is excellent, and above 70 is world-class. Always compare NPS scores within the same industry and methodology rather than against global averages.

How often should NPS surveys be sent?+

Relationship NPS surveys are typically sent quarterly or bi-annually. Transactional NPS surveys are triggered after specific events. Sending NPS surveys too frequently leads to survey fatigue and declining response rates.

Should NPS use a 0-10 or 1-10 scale?+

NPS must use a 0-10 scale. Using a 1-10 scale changes the structure and produces scores that cannot be benchmarked against standard NPS data.
