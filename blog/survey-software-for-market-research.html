Survey Software for Market Research: Qualtrics, Forsta, and Automation

Arun Raj•July 18, 2026•3 min read•Glossary
Definition

Market research survey automation converts quantitative research instruments from Word and Excel specification documents into Qualtrics, Forsta, QuestionPro, and other MR platforms - reducing survey programming turnaround from days to hours while maintaining the complex quota management, rotation logic, and timing structures that commercial research projects require.

Survey Challenges in Market Research

Speed-to-field pressure. Commercial market research operates under tight client timelines. A brand health tracker that misses its field window by a week disrupts the reporting cycle and may breach the client contract. Survey programming turnaround - the time from receiving a final survey script to having a live, tested survey in the field - is a critical bottleneck in most MR operations. Manual programming of a 25-question quantitative survey with routing, quotas, and piped text typically takes a programmer 4-8 hours. For agencies running 5-10 live projects simultaneously, programming backlog is the most common cause of fieldwork delays.

Complex survey logic and rotation. Market research surveys routinely include features that go beyond basic branching: randomisation of brand order, rotation of concept statements across monadic cells, loop questions that repeat a question block for each category used, quota management across multiple demographic and behavioural cells, and piped text that carries earlier responses into later questions. This logic is rarely captured explicitly in the survey script document - it is interpreted and scripted by the programmer from conventional MR shorthand. Translating this shorthand accurately and completely into the target platform requires experienced MR programmers who understand the conventions.

Multi-platform client requirements. MR agencies work with clients whose survey data must be delivered to specific platforms - some clients require Qualtrics, others Forsta, others SurveyMonkey or QuestionPro. The same survey may need to be deployed to different platforms for different markets in a global study. Agencies that manually program each platform variant face proportional increases in programming time and quality risk for each additional platform required.

Common Survey Types in Market Research

Brand tracking surveys - continuous or periodic measurement of brand awareness, consideration, preference, and image attributes across a target market, typically run quarterly or monthly with quota-balanced samples.

Usage and attitude (U&A) studies - foundational category research measuring penetration, frequency of use, occasion-based usage, brand imagery, and driver analysis across the competitive set.

Concept and product testing - monadic, sequential monadic, or paired comparison tests evaluating new product concepts, claims, pack designs, or advertising concepts against norms and competitive benchmarks.

Advertising pre-testing and post-testing - surveys measuring the impact of advertising creative on brand metrics, message recall, and purchase intent, often with video stimulus embedded in the survey.

Customer satisfaction and CX tracking - NPS, CSAT, and attribute satisfaction tracking for client CX programmes, often delivered by MR agencies as managed survey programmes rather than in-house by the client's CX team.

MaxDiff and conjoint studies - choice-based preference measurement studies requiring specialist survey design including randomised choice task presentation, balanced incomplete block design, and integrated analysis outputs.

Platforms MR Teams Use

Qualtrics is the most widely adopted platform in MR agencies globally, with strong support for quota management, loop and merge, and randomisation. Qualtrics' API enables panel integration with major online panels and its reporting tools support client dashboard delivery.

Forsta (formerly Confirmit and FocusVision merged) is a specialist MR platform with deep quantitative research capabilities including advanced loop structures, MaxDiff and conjoint support, and integrated qualitative research tools. Widely used by large global research agencies.

QuestionPro is used by mid-market MR agencies for quantitative research with quota management and panel integration. QuestionPro's research edition supports advanced survey logic and white-label client reporting.

Alchemer (formerly SurveyGizmo) is used by boutique MR agencies and research consultancies for flexible survey programming with robust API integration and custom scripting capabilities.

SurveyMonkey Audience and Enterprise are used for straightforward quantitative surveys where the panel access and ease of deployment outweigh the need for advanced MR features.
