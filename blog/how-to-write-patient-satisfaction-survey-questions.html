How to Write Patient Satisfaction Survey Questions: Examples and Guide

Arun Raj•July 18, 2026•3 min read•Glossary
Definition

A patient satisfaction survey measures patients' perceptions of their care experience across dimensions including communication, responsiveness, cleanliness, pain management, and discharge information. Patient satisfaction questions must balance clinical rigour, regulatory alignment, and accessibility for diverse patient populations.

Essential Questions to Include

Patient satisfaction surveys vary by care setting (inpatient, outpatient, emergency, primary care). The HCAHPS (Hospital Consumer Assessment of Healthcare Providers and Systems) survey is the standardised inpatient instrument mandated for CMS reporting in the US. For custom surveys, align questions with the HCAHPS dimensions for comparability.

Communication with doctors
"During this hospital stay, how often did doctors explain things in a way you could understand?" (Never / Sometimes / Usually / Always)
The HCAHPS uses a frequency scale, not a satisfaction scale, for most items. This distinction is important for programmes seeking HCAHPS benchmarking.

Communication with nurses
"During this hospital stay, how often did nurses listen carefully to you?"
"During this hospital stay, how often did nurses explain things in a way you could understand?"

Responsiveness of staff
"During this hospital stay, after you pressed the call button, how often did you get help as soon as you wanted it?"

Overall hospital rating
"Using any number from 0 to 10, where 0 is the worst hospital possible and 10 is the best hospital possible, what number would you use to rate this hospital during your stay?"
This is the HCAHPS overall rating question - equivalent to an NPS-style scale but distinct from NPS methodology.

Recommendation question
"Would you recommend this hospital to your friends and family?" (Definitely no / Probably no / Probably yes / Definitely yes)
The HCAHPS recommendation question uses a 4-point scale, not the standard NPS 0-10 scale.

Question Wording Best Practices

Patient satisfaction survey language must be accessible to respondents with varying health literacy levels. Write questions at a sixth-grade reading level wherever possible. Use everyday language rather than clinical terminology: "pain medicine" rather than "analgesics," "doctors explained" rather than "physicians communicated."

The HCAHPS frequency scale (Never / Sometimes / Usually / Always) is validated for patient populations and should be used for care process questions. Satisfaction scales are appropriate for overall ratings and hotel-like amenities (room cleanliness, food quality) but are not recommended for clinical care process questions where the HCAHPS approach has established benchmarks.

For custom outpatient or primary care surveys, specify the visit clearly in the survey header: "These questions are about your visit to [Clinic Name] on [Date]." This reduces confusion for patients who have multiple care relationships and ensures they are rating the specific interaction rather than their overall care history.

Platform Configuration

REDCap: REDCap is widely used in academic medical centres and clinical research for patient surveys. Use Radio Button fields for single-select items. The HCAHPS frequency scale maps to a 4-choice radio button field (Never, Sometimes, Usually, Always). Use the survey distribution module for email or SMS delivery. REDCap's data dictionary format enables batch import of all patient survey questions from a structured Excel template.

Qualtrics: Use the Multiple Choice question type for HCAHPS-format questions with the four-point frequency scale. Set up a Workflow to trigger the survey via SMS or email after discharge using the patient's contact information from the EHR system passed as embedded data. Use Qualtrics anonymisation settings to protect patient data in compliance with HIPAA requirements.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using a satisfaction scale for HCAHPS-benchmarked items. HCAHPS uses a frequency scale (Never to Always) for most care process questions. Switching to a satisfaction scale (Very Unsatisfied to Very Satisfied) breaks the benchmark link and produces scores that cannot be compared to national HCAHPS averages.

Surveying during the acute care episode. Surveying patients while they are still in the hospital or immediately after a difficult procedure introduces social desirability bias driven by dependency on the care team. Post-discharge surveying (typically 48 hours to 6 weeks post-discharge for HCAHPS) produces more honest responses.

Ignoring health literacy barriers. Patient satisfaction surveys using complex vocabulary or long sentences produce lower response rates and less reliable data from lower health literacy populations, which are often the populations whose care experiences most need to be captured.

Not excluding exclusion criteria populations. HCAHPS and most patient experience surveys exclude patients who died during the stay, patients discharged to hospice, patients with a psychiatric principal diagnosis, and patients under 18. Failing to apply exclusion criteria produces invalid benchmark submissions.

Frequently Asked Questions

What scale should patient satisfaction surveys use?+

HCAHPS uses a 4-point frequency scale (Never / Sometimes / Usually / Always) for care process questions and a 0-10 overall rating scale. Custom outpatient surveys often use a 5-point satisfaction scale. The key is matching the scale to established benchmarks for the care setting.

Do patient satisfaction surveys need to be HIPAA compliant?+

Yes. Patient satisfaction survey data that can be linked to an individual patient's healthcare record is PHI (Protected Health Information) and must be handled in compliance with HIPAA. Survey platforms used for patient surveys must have a BAA (Business Associate Agreement) in place with the healthcare organisation.

Can I use Google Forms for patient satisfaction surveys?+

Google Forms is not recommended for patient satisfaction surveys in regulated healthcare settings because it lacks the HIPAA-compliant infrastructure required for PHI. Qualtrics Healthcare, REDCap, and other platforms with signed BAAs are appropriate for patient survey data collection.
