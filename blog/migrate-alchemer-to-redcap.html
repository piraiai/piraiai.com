How to Migrate from Alchemer to REDCap: A Practical Guide

Arun Raj•July 18, 2026•4 min read

Migrating from Alchemer to REDCap typically happens for one reason: a research team that started data collection in Alchemer needs to move to REDCap for compliance reasons — usually HIPAA requirements, institutional data governance policies, or a grant requirement specifying REDCap as the collection platform.

Key Differences Between Alchemer and REDCap

Alchemer is a general-purpose survey platform with a visual interface and flexible logic. REDCap is a structured data capture system purpose-built for clinical and health sciences research. The structural differences matter for migration planning.

FeatureAlchemerREDCap
InterfaceVisual drag-and-drop builderOnline Designer and Data Dictionary
Data structureSurvey responses per submissionRecords with instruments and events
Field types30+ question types~15 field types including calculated fields
LogicRule-based conditionsBranching logic per field
ComplianceSOC 2, GDPRHIPAA, 21 CFR Part 11, FISMA
Audit trailLimitedFull audit trail built in

The Migration Process

1

Export your Alchemer survey

Export your survey from Alchemer in Word or PDF format. If the survey has existing response data you need to migrate, export that separately as a CSV for manual import into REDCap after the instrument is set up.

2

Convert to REDCap Data Dictionary

Upload the exported survey to platform.piraiai.com and select REDCap as the target. Pirai AI generates a REDCap Data Dictionary CSV with the correct field types, variable names, field labels, and choice values mapped from your Alchemer survey.

3

Create a new REDCap project

In REDCap, create a new empty project. Go to Project Setup, then Data Dictionary, and upload the CSV. REDCap validates the file and shows a summary of fields to be created. Click Commit Changes to apply.

4

Review in the Online Designer

After the upload, review all instruments in the REDCap Online Designer. Check field types, choice labels, and instrument organisation. Add branching logic using the branching logic editor, referencing your pre-migration logic documentation.

REDCap field type mapping: Alchemer's checkbox questions map to REDCap checkbox fields. Single-answer questions map to radio buttons or dropdowns. Open-ended text maps to text or notes fields. Numeric questions should use REDCap's text field with number validation applied.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why would a research team migrate from Alchemer to REDCap?+

The most common reason is compliance. REDCap is purpose-built for clinical and health sciences research with HIPAA-compliant infrastructure, full audit trails, and institutional server hosting. Teams collecting health data or receiving grants that specify REDCap as the required platform need to migrate regardless of their existing Alchemer setup.

Can I convert my Alchemer survey to a REDCap Data Dictionary automatically?+

Yes. Upload your Alchemer survey document to Pirai AI and select REDCap as the target. Pirai AI generates a REDCap Data Dictionary CSV with field types, variable names, field labels, and choice values mapped from your Alchemer survey, ready to upload via REDCap Project Setup.

What Alchemer question types do not map directly to REDCap?+

Alchemer slider questions do not have a direct REDCap equivalent and are typically converted to text fields with numeric validation. Alchemer's matrix questions map to individual REDCap fields rather than a grid. Complex Alchemer logic needs to be rebuilt as REDCap branching logic after the Data Dictionary import.

Is REDCap HIPAA compliant?+

REDCap is hosted on institutional servers, which means compliance depends on how your institution has configured and validated its REDCap installation. Most academic medical centres and research institutions run HIPAA-compliant REDCap environments. Confirm with your REDCap administrator that the project will be hosted on a validated, HIPAA-compliant server before beginning data collection.
