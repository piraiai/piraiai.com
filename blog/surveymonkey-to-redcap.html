How to Migrate from SurveyMonkey to REDCap

Arun Raj • June 1, 2026 • 2 min read

The most common reason teams migrate from SurveyMonkey to REDCap is compliance. SurveyMonkey's HIPAA-compliant plan exists but comes at significant cost and with limitations. REDCap is free for qualifying institutions, built specifically for clinical and research data collection, and provides audit trails, access controls, and data governance that SurveyMonkey cannot match at the instrument level. Moving surveys between the two platforms is not straightforward : but Pirai AI makes it a single-step conversion.

Why Teams Migrate from SurveyMonkey to REDCap

ReasonDetail
HIPAA complianceREDCap is HIPAA-compliant by design for clinical and patient data collection
IRB requirementsInstitutional review boards often require REDCap for sensitive research data
Audit trailsREDCap provides field-level audit logs required for clinical trials and regulated research
CostREDCap is free for qualifying research institutions vs SurveyMonkey enterprise pricing
Longitudinal data collectionREDCap supports repeated measures and longitudinal study designs natively

Step-by-Step: SurveyMonkey to REDCap

  • 1
    Export your SurveyMonkey surveyIn SurveyMonkey, export your survey as Word (.docx) or PDF. This preserves question text and answer options in a readable format.
  • 2
    Upload to Pirai AI and select REDCapGo to platform.piraiai.com, upload your exported file, and select REDCap. Pirai AI generates a Data Dictionary .csv with all required REDCap columns populated.
  • 3
    Create a new empty REDCap projectLog in to REDCap, click New Project, select Purpose (typically Research), choose Empty project, and click Create Project.
  • 4
    Upload the Data Dictionary and commitGo to Project Setup > Data Dictionary. Click Choose File, select your .csv, click Upload File. Review the summary and click Commit Changes.
  • 5
    Review in the Online DesignerGo to the Online Designer to verify all fields. Add validation rules, branching logic, and access controls as required by your study protocol.

What does not migrate: SurveyMonkey skip logic, collector settings, and response data do not transfer. REDCap branching logic, field validation, and calculated fields must be configured manually in the Online Designer after import.
