Survey Software for Government: Public Consultation and Staff Surveys

Arun Raj•July 18, 2026•3 min read•Glossary
Definition

Government and public sector survey automation converts public consultation instruments, citizen satisfaction surveys, and staff engagement forms from Word or Excel documents into Microsoft Forms, Qualtrics, Google Forms, and other platforms - enabling accessible, consistent deployment at the scale required for public administration programmes.

Survey Challenges in Government

Accessibility requirements. Government surveys in most jurisdictions must meet accessibility standards - WCAG 2.1 AA in the UK and EU, Section 508 in the US - to ensure equitable access for citizens with disabilities. This requires surveys to be operable via screen reader, keyboard navigable, and designed with sufficient colour contrast and label clarity. Not all survey platforms meet these standards by default, and surveys built manually by non-specialist staff frequently fail accessibility audits. Survey instruments that originate as Word documents need to be configured correctly in the target platform to meet these requirements, not just structurally converted.

Public consultation scale and volume management. Government consultations and surveys can receive thousands to hundreds of thousands of responses in a short window. Public consultations on major policy changes - planning frameworks, transport projects, legislative proposals - require survey platforms that can handle high concurrent response volumes, with data stored in government-controlled environments rather than shared cloud infrastructure. Survey programming at this scale, with branching logic for different respondent types (individuals, organisations, businesses), is complex and time-consuming when done manually.

Multi-language requirements. Federal, national, and municipal governments serving diverse populations frequently need surveys in multiple languages simultaneously. Managing consistent translation of question wording, scale labels, and response options across languages while maintaining the same survey structure in the target platform requires careful coordination that is error-prone in manual processes.

Common Survey Types in Government

Public consultations - statutory and non-statutory consultations on policy proposals, planning applications, service changes, and legislative reviews, open to citizens, organisations, and businesses with different question routing for each respondent type.

Citizen satisfaction surveys - measuring satisfaction with public services including local authority services, national government services, licensing, and benefits administration. Often benchmarked against national or regional citizen satisfaction indices.

Service user feedback surveys - short post-interaction surveys triggered after specific government service touchpoints: benefit claims, licence applications, planning applications, court interactions, and health service appointments.

Staff engagement surveys - annual and pulse surveys for civil servants and public sector employees, often benchmarked against the Civil Service People Survey (UK) or equivalent national frameworks. Running across large, geographically dispersed workforces.

Programme evaluation surveys - surveys measuring the outcomes of government programmes, grants, and interventions for reporting to legislators, auditors, and funding bodies.

Census and population surveys - large-scale structured data collection instruments requiring high volume capacity, multi-mode deployment (online, paper, phone), and rigorous data validation.

Platforms Government Teams Use

Microsoft Forms is widely deployed across government organisations using Microsoft 365. It integrates with SharePoint and Teams for internal surveys and supports basic public-facing surveys. Microsoft Forms data is stored in Microsoft 365 government cloud environments (GCC High in the US, sovereign cloud in the EU) satisfying data residency requirements.

Qualtrics Government provides FedRAMP-authorised cloud infrastructure for US federal agencies, with equivalents in other jurisdictions. Used for more complex surveys requiring advanced branching, quota management, and dashboard reporting capabilities beyond Microsoft Forms' scope.

Google Forms is used by some local government and public sector teams within Google Workspace for Education or Government deployments, particularly for internal surveys and programme feedback.

SmartSurvey is widely used in UK government (NHS, local councils, central government departments) for public-facing consultation surveys, with UK data residency and Cyber Essentials Plus certification.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which survey platform is best for government public consultations?+

The best platform depends on the jurisdiction and data residency requirements. UK government teams frequently use SmartSurvey or Qualtrics (with UK data residency). US federal agencies use FedRAMP-authorised platforms including Qualtrics Government. Local councils and state/provincial governments often use Microsoft Forms within their existing Microsoft 365 government cloud environment.

Do government surveys need to meet accessibility standards?+

Yes. Most jurisdictions require government-facing surveys to meet web accessibility standards - WCAG 2.1 AA in the UK and EU, Section 508 in the US. The survey platform must be inherently accessible, and surveys must be configured with clear labels, sufficient colour contrast, and keyboard navigation support. Accessibility should be verified before public launch.

Can Pirai AI convert public consultation documents to Microsoft Forms?+

Yes. Upload your consultation document to Pirai AI and select Microsoft Forms as the target platform. The question structure, branching logic for different respondent types, and answer options are converted to Microsoft Forms format. Complex branching is implemented as section routing, which is Microsoft Forms' supported branching approach.

How does Pirai AI handle surveys with different question routes for citizens vs organisations?+

Question routing based on respondent type (individual vs organisation) is handled in Pirai AI through the source document's branching structure. In Microsoft Forms, this routes to different sections. In Qualtrics, it uses display logic conditions. The routing logic is identified from the source document and mapped to the target platform's branching mechanism.
